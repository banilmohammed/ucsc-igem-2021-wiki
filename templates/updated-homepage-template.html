An Antibiotic Free Method of Eliminating Harmful Genes

Manure has been shown to contain high loads of broad-host-range plasmids, promoting the transfer of antibiotic resistant genes via horizontal gene transfer.

Around 600 million people around the world fall ill due to foodbore illness.

The Problem

California is the leading producer of leafy green vegetables, contributing roughly __ a year to our state's economy, and providing jobs for __ % of people in the workforce. Other products such as ground beef play an important role in the economy as well, with a profit of __ dollars. Production of vegetables and meat opens the door for many pathogens in cattle and soil to be transmitted to humans, who may fall ill from the consumption of contaminated foods.

One main threat to human health is STEC, which comes from consumption of contaminated lettuce and meat products, causing diarrhea and Hemolytic Uremic Syndrome (HUS), a potentially fatal kidney disorder. STEC is one of the top 3 food borne illnesses, due to the high demand for greens and meat, but the detection and prevention of contaminated food is hard to manage on such a large scale. Seeing as we are a California team in an agriculture-centric area, it became important for us to look into the threats of foodborne illness coming from our own backyard in hopes to prevent outbreaks at the source of the issue.

In recent years, antibiotic resistance has become an increasingly harmful issue in medical and agricultural settings, threatening human health and leaving few alternative treatments for conditions that require antibiotics. As recently as April of this year, the World Health Organization revealed that “ none of the 43 antibiotics that are currently in clinical development sufficiently address the problem of drug resistance in the world’s most dangerous bacteria,” and further, “82% of the recently approved antibiotics [are] derivatives of existing antibiotic classes with well-established drug-resistance.” This issue will only become greater as antibiotics are administered in higher dosages to combat these resistant bacteria.

Our Solution

We have designed a solution to disarming pathogenic bacteria without the need for antibiotics. By creating a site-specific gene targeting system that utilizes INTEGRATE -- an RNA-guided transposition technique -- we are creating a solution that could be adapted to target genes in many different bacterial species, lessening the possibility of foodborne illness, and slowing the spread of antimicrobial resistance.
